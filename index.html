CASE 01

Started YouTube podcasts from sub-zero and gained 10.8M views in 18 months.

When I joined Scripbox in Sep 2024, the channel needed a content engine — fresh strategy, weekly publishing, and a story arc the algorithm would reward. Small team. Even smaller editing budget.

my move

Built a content engine across long-form (podcasts) and Shorts; designed each long-form episode to clip into 4–6 Shorts; A/B-tested thumbnails on tentpole episodes; ran a weekly performance review and re-cut underperformers.

result
  • Long-form: 6.1M views · 27.1M impressions · 4.6% CTR · 2:17 avg view duration
  • Shorts: 4.7M views · 3.5M engaged views · 160.8K likes · +8K subs
  • Channel total: ~10.8M views · +30.1K subscribers over 18 months
what I learned

A long-form-first, Shorts-fed strategy compounds. Every podcast became its own distribution machine — one recording session, then 4–6 vertical clips, then a LinkedIn cut, then a WhatsApp carousel.

CASE 02

One podcast episode → 410K views, +7,100 subscribers.

Wanted to break out of niche-finance reach and prove the podcast could pull a general-audience number. Picked an internal guest with broad name-recognition, trust, & experience, with a clear hook — "Top Financial Advisor Reveals Best Mutual Fund Strategies."

my move

Tight 6:17 average-view-duration cut. A/B-tested 3 thumbnail variants in YouTube Studio for 10 days. Rotated the winner permanently.

result
  • 410.9K views · 7.8M impressions · 3.8% CTR
  • +7,100 subscribers from a single episode
  • 43.1K watch hours · 6:17 average view duration · 292K unique viewers
what I learned

The winning thumbnail accounted for a 4-point lift in watch-time share. Two-line, contrast-heavy thumbnails consistently outperform single-line or pure-graphic variants for this audience.

CASE 03

A/B-testing thumbnails to lift watch-time share by 2–4 points.

Click-through is the leverage point for YouTube — small thumbnail wins compound across hundreds of thousands of views. This is the process case study: what makes me different from a candidate who just records and posts.

my move

For every tentpole episode, I shoot three thumbnail variants with different framing and copy. I use YouTube Studio's built-in A/B test for 7–10 days, rotate the winner permanently, and track the watch-time share lift per test in a Sheet.

result
  • Winning variants consistently lifted watch-time share by 2–4 percentage points
  • Compounded across 100K+ views per episode = real lift in subscriber conversion
  • Most-used winning patterns: faces > graphics, two-line copy > one, yellow > red for this audience
what I learned

Thumbnails are a system, not a guess. Once you've run 10+ tests, you have a reusable playbook — and the next episode launches with the winning pattern already baked in.

CASE 04

The LinkedIn voice that hit 10–15× the B2B benchmark.

B2B finance LinkedIn typically engages at 2–5%. We wanted thought-leadership reach without paid push. I architected hooks using a custom Claude skills setup, carousel-first format, and a founder-aligned point of view.

my move

Hook → controversial-but-defensible POV → carousel proof → soft CTA. Published at the hour the founder's audience was most active. Then iterated weekly based on what reactions vs. clicks vs. reposts each post got.

result
  • Top post: 47.56% ER · 46.01% CTR · 584 engagements / 565 clicks on 1,228 impressions
  • Scaled version: 46.94% ER · 44.65% CTR · 5,480 engagements / 5,213 clicks on 11.6K impressions
  • Multiple posts in the 8–11% range — still 2–3× the B2B benchmark
what I learned

The pattern transfers across topics. Once you know what your audience over-rewards (POV + proof + the right line break), every post starts at 2× baseline before you've even hit publish.

CASE 05

One Reel → 30.6K reach, 180 new followers.

Looking for what made the Instagram algorithm push us to non-followers. The answer was emotional hooks for finance content — even more than informational ones.

my move

Emotional-arc opener ("My husband Sanjay passed away…") for a Reel about life insurance and inheritance gaps — wrote, recorded, edited, and cross-posted to Facebook for incremental distribution.

result
  • 37,060 views · 30,689 accounts reached
  • 276 likes · 150 saves (saves = intent signal)
  • 180 profile follows from a single post
what I learned

Emotional hooks for finance content drive saves better than informational hooks — and saves correlate with follow-through far better than likes. Cross-posting to Facebook sometimes outperforms IG on the same Reel.

CASE 06

WhatsApp channel: 0 → 3K subscribers in under 3 months.

Scripbox needed a faster distribution surface than newsletters for market-news POV. Founded the channel on Feb 24, 2026 and built a Mon/Thu cadence from scratch.

my move

Co-developed POV with the founder; designed easy-digest carousel infographics with minimal text and one stat per slide; kept the cadence ruthlessly consistent.

result
  • 0 → 3K subscribers in ~2.5 months
  • 100% on-cadence delivery — never missed a Mon/Thu drop
  • Carousel-with-one-stat format outperformed text-heavy POV every time
what I learned

WhatsApp rewards visual hierarchy. One number, one line, big contrast — that's the unit of attention people will swipe through. Anything more and they drop off.
